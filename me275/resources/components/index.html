Advice for Ordering Components

  • Don’t overpay for your components. Here’s an estimate of how much each of your components should cost:
    • Batteries: $5-10 each
    • ESCs: ~$10 each
    • Motors: $10-25 each
    • Propellers: Varies, but generally inexpensive

  • Make sure you minimize shipping costs. Ideally, you shouldn’t have to pay for shipping at all. I recommend you batch your orders together (i.e., order all your components from the same place). If your order is high enough in value, it may qualify for free shipping (>$50 Hobbyking orders from the U.S. Warehouse qualify for free shipping). Consider combining your order with another group if you don’t qualify for free shipping.

  • Ensure that shipping times are reasonable for the merchant you choose. Some hobby websites are not very efficient at getting components shipped. Others ship from China and we don’t have time to wait that long. Make sure you know the time frame for shipping so you can get your components within the next week or two. Also, do not order backordered items. They will not arrive in time. Components from the Hobbyking U.S. warehouse, using standard shipping, generally take about a week from when the order is placed.

  • Unless your plane is terribly inefficient (and hopefully it is not after all this analysis) you shouldn’t need a huge battery. It’ll just add extra weight. A good size is probably somewhere between 500 mAh and 1500 mAh, depending on the size of your plane. Based on your estimated power requirements you should be able to predict roughly how much power you actually will need.

  • You can get battery connectors and servos at the EE Shop.

  • Make sure you know how you’re mounting your propeller to your motor. Some motors come with components for this, other’s don’t. You may need to buy a propeller adapter to attach your propeller to your motor.

  • For purchasing multiple RC aircraft components we generally recommend Hobbyking for value and selection. If you only need a single component there’s a hobby store in Orem (HobbyTown USA), but their prices are higher. Amazon is sometimes an option but the selection is more limited and quality is sometimes questionable.
