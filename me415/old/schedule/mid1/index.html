Midterm 1

The midterm is a take-home timed exam administered via Learning Suite. The exam is open book/notes/calculator and focuses on demonstrating understanding of important fundamental concepts. You will have up to 3 hours. The exam will be available on Learning Suite starting after class on Friday and must be completed before midnight next Wednesday (10/13/2021). You can take it anytime you wish during that interval, but must do so in one sitting.

A not necessarily exhaustive list of topics we have discussed:

  • nature of the standard atmosphere
  • pressure, density, temperature, speed of sound, viscosity, etc.
  • optimal altitudes and why
  • tradeoffs in skin friction and pressure drag
  • read drag polars and lift curve slopes
  • interpret pressure distributions: favorable and adverse pressure gradients. separation.
  • NACA sections
  • main results of thin airfoil theory
  • different components of drag and where they come from
  • laminar vs turbulent
  • estimate parasitic drag for lifting surfaces and bodies of revolution
  • how to compute induced drag and what it depends on
  • what is inviscid span efficiency and what is ideal
  • difference between inviscid span efficiency and oswald efficiency factor
  • normal and oblique shocks and when they occur and why they occur
  • results of simple sweep theory
  • how do the different parameters affect wing design
  • what changes to make to improve lift and cl distribution. What is desirable.
  • what is critical section theory
  • criteria for static stability
  • center of pressure vs aerodynamic center
  • static stability and trim.
  • static margin
  • different approaches to longitudinal stability (flying wing, tail, canard)
  • what tail volume coefficients are
  • identify dynamic modes of aircraft and their basic character. How to affect design.
  • compute stall speed
