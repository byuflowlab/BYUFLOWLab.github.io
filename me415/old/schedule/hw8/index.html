HW 8: Controls

due 11/15/2017 before midnight via Learning Suite 50 possible points

The first two questions will be worked on collectively as a team, whereas the last question asks about your specific contributions. For the team parts you should work collectively on one report, but each individual should submit the report so its easier for the TAs to assign credit. It is not expected that every person works on every part of the autopilot development (it would be wiser to split up tasks), but it is expected that all contribute meaningfully to the autopilot development in some way. You should spend at least 5.5 hours on your project, leaving an hour for the quiz and a small amount of assigned reading.

  1. Provide a detailed description of your control strategy. We don’t want to look through your raw code, but words alone won’t be clear enough either. You might consider using psuedocode, flowcharts, or other types of diagrams (along with words) to clearly describe your control strategy. If you have multiple controllers then describe each (i.e., one for altitude, one for navigation). For each controller, describe the parameter you will target and exactly how will you compute this parameter from your sensors (if it is not directly given by the sensors). Describe the logic of your controller. Describe any filters, limiters, or other strategies you will use.

    A template for your controller is available here: https://github.com/byuflowlab/autopilot-me415 in autopilot.py (Python).

  2. Provide a detailed description of the testing strategy you have employed to date. Some suggested testing approaches include:
    • flying RC with data collection on to check typical control inputs and responses
    • dynamic simulation of your controller (i.e., using Gazebo)
    • on-the-ground testing of your controller (i.e., walking around with your plane with the autopilot on)
    • in-flight autopilot testing

    Show your test data and clearly describe the results. Describe what you’ve learned and the improvements you have made.

    To use Gazebo you’ll need to modify the plane_params.yaml file available at the same link https://github.com/byuflowlab/autopilot-me415. You should have all the information already except for the control derivatives. These are like the stability derivatives except they are the derivatives of the forces/moments with respect to control inputs (rudder, elevator, etc.). You can estimate these with XFLR5 also. The relevant tutorials are:

    • Flapped foil starting at 4:00
    • Flapped wing
    • Control derivatives
  3. Report:
    • The amount of time you spent working on autopilot development (or other relevant project tasks).
    • Describe your specific contributions this week in detail.
