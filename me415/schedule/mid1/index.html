Preliminary Design Review and Midterm 1

Your homework for this week is to prepare for the design review, prepare for the midterm, and take the midterm.

Preliminary Design Review

Put together a presentation for the design review. The grade is based on providing sufficient information to understand the design but is not based on the quality of the aircraft design or on slide quality. The purpose of the review is to have a dialogue to allow for helpful feedback and answering of questions. In other words, you donâ€™t need to spend time making fancy slides, but you do need to present key information clearly so that we can have a meaningful discussion. At a minimum, I recommend including the following:

  • Multiple views of your design and your overarching configuration rationale.
  • Estimated total weight, the weight of key components, and the center of mass location.
  • Estimated lift to drag ratio.
  • Design speed and stall speed.
  • Airfoil design and rationale including lift curve, drag polar, and moment curve.
  • Wing design and rationale including lift and cl distributions.
  • Tail and Fuselage design and rationale including open loop stability plot, static margin, and key stability derivatives.
  • Any other unique design aspects.

We will split the teams into three groups between myself and the two TAs. Your team will have 15 minutes for the in-class review.

Midterm 1

The midterm is a take-home timed exam administered via Learning Suite. The exam is open book/notes/calculator and focuses on demonstrating understanding of important fundamental concepts. You will have up to 3 hours. The exam will be available on Learning Suite starting after class on Friday and must be completed before midnight next Wednesday (10/16/2019). You can take it anytime you wish during that interval, but must do so in one sitting.

A not necessarily exhaustive list of topics we have discussed:

  • nature of the standard atmosphere
  • pressure, density, temperature, speed of sound, viscosity, etc.
  • optimal altitudes and why
  • tradeoffs in skin friction and pressure drag
  • read drag polars and lift curve slopes
  • interpret pressure distributions: favorable and adverse pressure gradients. separation.
  • NACA sections
  • main results of thin airfoil theory
  • different components of drag and where they come from
  • laminar vs turbulent

  • estimate parasitic drag for lifting surfaces and bodies of revolution

  • how to compute induced drag and what it depends on
  • what is inviscid span efficiency and what is ideal
  • difference between inviscid span efficiency and oswald efficiency factor
  • normal and oblique shocks and when they occur and why they occur
  • results of simple sweep theory
  • how do the different parameters affect wing design
  • what changes to make to improve lift and cl distribution. What is desirable.
  • what is critical section theory
  • criteria for static stability
  • center of pressure vs aerodynamic center
  • static stability and trim.
  • static margin
  • different approaches to longitudinal stability (flying wing, tail, canard)
  • what tail volume coefficients are
  • identify dynamic modes of aircraft and their basic character. How to affect design.
  • compute stall speed
