Critical Design Review and Midterm 2

Your homework for this week is to prepare for the design review, prepare for the midterm, and take the midterm.

Critical Design Review

Same format as last time. The main focus will be on providing feedback and suggestions on your built airplane and any flight testing to date. Some elements you will need to prepare:

  • Your airplane (bring it with you). It should be a complete initial version. Discuss challenges, areas of improvement, and any questions you have.
  • Video(s) of the aircraft in flight (even if just a simple glide test from one person to another). Discuss observed performance from flight testing.
  • Any design details you want to discuss. We aren’t looking for anything specific here. If you’ve had significant redesign, or design aspects you’re concerned about it would be to discuss them.
  • Telemetry testing and any questions you have. If you have in-flight performance data, bring that.
  • A schedule/plan for the rest of the semester.

Midterm 2

Same format as last time. Take-home via Learning Suite, open book/notes/calculator, 3 hour time limit. The exam will be available on Learning Suite starting after class on Friday and must be completed by midnight on Wednesday (11/20/2019). You can take it anytime you wish during that interval, but must do so in one sitting.

The exam will focus on the topics since the last midterm: propulsion, performance, structures (no rockets), however it is comprehensive.

A not necessarily exhaustive list of topics we have discussed since the last midterm:

  • DC motor model.
  • Analyzing propeller data, thrust, torque, efficiency.
  • Designing a well matched motor/prop/battery combination. Understanding the effects of key parameters.
  • Thrust equation, what the terms mean and when each applies.
  • Understanding tradeoffs in max thrust and efficiency.
  • Differences and rationale behind turboprops, turbofans, turbojets.
  • Why compressor and turbine blades are shaped like they are.
  • What specific fuel consumption is
  • Main tradeoffs in tractor/pusher, single/twin
  • Brueget range equation and how to use
  • Range for electric powered aircraft and key differences from a fuel consuming aircraft
  • Endurance meaning and equations.
  • Insight into how optimal speeds vary for max range vs max endurance vs max L/D
  • Rate of climb: key metrics
  • Glide ratio: how to determine gliding distance, or conversely L/D.
  • Turning radius and key parameters.
  • Effect of slats vs flaps
  • How to determine a balanced takeoff field length
  • Important parameters in affecting landing field length.
  • What spoilers are and what their function is.
  • What a semi-monocoque structure is and why it is used.
  • Relationship between thickness and wing weight for a bending constrained wing.
  • Lift centroid and how that affects tradeoffs in aerodynamics and structures, including optimal inviscid span efficiencies.
  • How to read/construct a speed-altitude flight envelope and a V-n diagram.
  • Differences between airspeeds (e.g., true airspeed vs equivalent airspeed) and why they are used.
