Critical Design Review

Ideally, you should have a fully assembled aircraft that is ready for the Fly OFf, but at a minimum you need a mostly assembled aircraft and to have performed one glide test prior to the CDR. The form of the glide test(s) may vary depending on what stage you are at. First tests are usually just a short toss from one person to another. If that is all your design is ready for, that is sufficient for this assignment. As you adjust the trim and design of your aircraft those tests will progress to further distances. Eventually, you’ll want to test landing on the ground. Having servos installed can speed up the test progression since you can control the trim during flight and soften landings. In that case, throwing from a hill is often desirable to allow for more response time.

Similar format to last time. The main focus will be on providing feedback and suggestions on your built airplane and any flight testing to date.

Some elements you will need to prepare:

  • Your airplane (bring it with you). Discuss challenges, areas of improvement, and any questions you have.
  • Video of your glide test and any other videos you might have of the aircraft in flight. Discuss observed performance from flight testing.
  • Any design details you want to discuss. We aren’t looking for anything specific here. If you’ve had significant redesign, or design aspects you’re concerned about it would be good to discuss them.
  • A plan for remaining building/testing leading up to the Fly Off.
