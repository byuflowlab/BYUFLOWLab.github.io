Project Report

due 12/12/2019 before midnight via Learning Suite 175 possible points

Format

Your report must be submitted in PDF format. The cover page should contain your team name, the names of all team members, and a nice photo (or rendering) of your aircraft. Refer to the BYU ME Writing Materials for general writing guidance. Here is a past example report that scored well (note the mission and requirements were not exactly the same).

Note that in addition to the design details your report needs to include details from the fly off. These are separated into two rubrics to highlight than you could get all the design points regardless of how well your aircraft performs.

Design Rubric (125 points)

Design (40%):

  • [5 pts] Overall configuration choices are appropriate and soundly justified.
  • [5 pts] Airfoil selection is appropriate and soundly justified through good analysis and discussion (including comparisons to other airfoils, and multiple key metrics).
  • [5 pts] Wing design is appropriate and soundly justified through good analysis and discussion (including appropriate lift and cl plots).
  • [5 pts] Predicted and actual weights of all components are shown with discussion on how it affected the design if applicable.
  • [5 pts] Tail/canard/sweep sizing (including tail incidence) is appropriate and soundly justified through analysis and discussion.
  • [5 pts] A proper static and dynamic stability analysis is shown with discussion on how it affected the design.
  • [5 pts] An analysis of the propulsion system and component selection (motor, battery, propeller, esc) is included (whether or not actually used). System is efficient and suited to the aircraft.
  • [5 pts] Overall performance is shown with reasonable results (range, endurance, L/D, cruise speed and CL, CLmax, climb rate). Performance metrics are appropriate.
  • [5 pts] Fully dimensioned aircraft with a 3-views (front/side/top drawing).
  • [5 pts] Overall quality of design analysis.
  • [+5 pts] (extra credit) Exceptional analysis and/or design novelty.

Build/Test/Fly (40%):

  • [5 pts] Evidence of good building technique.
  • [5 pts] Evidence of an improved design through lessoned learned while building.
  • [5 pts] Evidence of a built aircraft that (reasonably) matches the design.
  • [5 pts] Evidence of good flight testing and observations from flight testing.
  • [5 pts] Evidence of improvements made from flight testing.
  • [10 pts] Mission attempt(s) clearly described and documented (videos or links to videos are welcome!).
  • [5 pts] Good description/analysis of glide tests (distance) and corresponding L/D.
  • [5 pts] Reasonable analysis/match for glide performance (L/D comparison for flight vs computation) with discussion on discrepancies.
  • [5 pts] Good discussion on areas of improvement if you were to do it all again.
  • [+5 pts] (extra credit) Exceptional build techniques and/or innovation.

Writing (20%):

  • [5 pts] Writing is clear and cohesive.
  • [5 pts] Report is well organized.
  • [5 pts] Figures/data are nicely presented.
  • [5 pts] Level of detail is appropriate.
  • [5 pts] Good conclusions and reflections on the process.

Performance Rubric (50 points)

Your performance will be judged only by what is included in the report, so be sure to provide good evidence of the following. Videos or links to videos can be particularly helpful.

  • [15 pts] Completed the mission while respecting boundary and ceiling limits (regardless of how well it is completed).
  • [10 pts] Reached targets at par.
  • [10 pts] Aircraft clearly handled well (e.g., stability, gliding).
  • [10 pts] Aircraft achieved good lift-to-drag ratio as evidenced by glide test.
  • [5 pts] Evidence that good safety practices were followed.
  • [+5 pts] (extra credit) Impressive performance.

Peer Assessment

Provide an assessment of the contribution level for yourself and the rest of your team members. In the submission notes of Learning Suite, list each team member’s name including yourself, and next to each name list a number using the scale below. You may provide additional comments if needed.

  • 3: actively engaged. strong contributions.
  • 2: somewhat engaged. some contributions.
  • 1: not very engaged. few contributions.
