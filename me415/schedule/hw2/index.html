HW 2: Drag and Airfoils

due 9/27/2017 before midnight via Learning Suite 50 possible points

  1. Create a function to compute the total drag of your aircraft during steady, level flight.

    Create a plot of the lift-to-drag ratio (L/D) as a function of flight speed. Find the speed that maximizes L/D (note this may not the best long-term metric, but it uses what you know at this stage to provide a rough estimate of the optimal flight speed). Report the design flight speed (speed that maximizes L/D), the corresponding Reynolds number, the corresponding lift coefficient, and the max L/D. Check that your numbers are reasonable.

    Aside: It may be instructive to visualize the two main components of drag as a function of flight speed.

  2. Assuming compressibility drag is negligible, derive an analytic expression in terms of AR, e, CDp for:
    • the lift coefficient that maximizes L/D
    • the corresponding max L/D

    Using the values for AR, e, and CDp for your aircraft and its design speed, compare what you found numerically for both CL and L/D with what you find in this simple analytic expression. If the values are different explain why.
