HW 2: Drag and Airfoils

due 9/27/2017 before midnight via Learning Suite 50 possible points

Project

  1. Setup a routine to compute the total drag of your aircraft during steady, level flight.

    Create a plot of the lift-to-drag ratio (L/D) as a function of flight speed. Find the speed that maximizes L/D (note this is probably not the best long-term metric, but it uses what you know at this stage to provide a rough estimate of the optimal flight speed). Report the design flight speed (speed that maximizes L/D), the corresponding lift coefficient, and the max L/D. Check that your numbers are reasonable.

    Aside: It may be instructive to visualize the two main components of drag as a function of flight speed.

  2. Report the design Reynolds number and lift coefficient for your airfoil (note that the wing coefficient and airfoil coefficient are not generally the same). Compare the performance of at least three different airfoils designed for your Reynolds number using XFOIL. Plot their performance on drag polars and lift curves. Select (or design) an airfoil that is appropriate for your aircraft and plot it.

General

  1. Repeat the same calculations (i.e., in the optimal altitude), but for the supersonic business jet proposed by Aerion at its supersonic cruise speed. Remember to include compressibility drag!
