HW 3: Better Drag Analysis and Wing Design

due 9/25/2024 before midnight via Learning Suite 25 possible points

  1. Now that we can evaluate the aerodynamic performance of airfoils we can improve our drag estimation. For this analysis we will use the Douglas DC-3, a very successful plane that helped make air travel popular in the U.S. Some key specs, dimensions, and airfoil schedule are below.

    wing span 29 m
    takeoff mass 11,000 kg
    cruise speed 93 m/s
    cruise altitude 10,000 ft

    • Compute the induced drag (inviscid portion of lift-dependent drag). Assume an inviscid span efficiency of 0.98 (nearly elliptic).
    • Compute the viscous drag (combination of parasitic drag and viscous lift-dependent drag) by integrating the airfoil drag across the wing. For our purposes we will only use the 5 spanwise locations shown in the figure (a trapezoidal integration should suffice). The lift coefficient at the five stations from root to tip are \(c_l = 0.275, 0.266, 0.317, 0.363, 0.0\). To find the drag coefficients you will need to use an airfoil analysis (i.e., XFLR5).
