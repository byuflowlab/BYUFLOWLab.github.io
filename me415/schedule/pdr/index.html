Preliminary Design Review

Put together a presentation for the design review. The grade is based on providing sufficient information to understand the design but is not based on the quality of the aircraft design or on slide quality. The purpose of the review is to have a dialogue to allow for helpful feedback and answering of questions. In other words, you donâ€™t need to spend time making fancy slides, but you do need to present key information clearly so that we can have a meaningful discussion. At a minimum, I recommend including the following:

  • Multiple views of your design and your overarching configuration rationale.
  • Estimated total weight, the weight of key components, and the center of mass location.
  • Estimated lift to drag ratio.
  • Design speed and stall speed.
  • Airfoil design and rationale including lift curve, drag polar, and moment curve.
  • Wing design and rationale including lift and cl distributions.
  • Tail and Fuselage design and rationale including open loop stability plot, static margin, and key stability derivatives.
  • Any other unique design aspects.

We will split the teams into three groups between myself and the two TAs. Your team will have 15 minutes for the in-class review.
